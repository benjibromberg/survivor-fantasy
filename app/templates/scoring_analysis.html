The Bottom Line

How We Tested

For each scoring configuration, we simulated random snake drafts across 7 player counts (4–10) and 9 seasons, then tracked full leaderboard trajectories episode-by-episode. Every player drafts exactly 4 castaways. When there are too many players for one pool, balanced sub-drafts split the group so everyone still gets 4 picks (castaways can be shared across sub-drafts). Wildcards are picked after the first elimination and can overlap other players' draft picks.

Each configuration was scored on twelve metrics, combined into a single composite fun/fairness score (higher = better):

Configs with high variance across drafts are penalized (consistency penalty on comeback rate, suspense, draft skill, and final spread standard deviations). Parameter space covered using two-phase optimization: stratified random sampling (25,000 configs) followed by iterative neighborhood refinement of top results. All point values snapped to 0.5 increments.
