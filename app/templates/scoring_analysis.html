Scoring Analysis

We tested 3,000+ scoring configurations across 9 modern US Survivor seasons (41–49) with 3,150 simulated random drafts (4–10 players each) to find settings that are fun, fair, and competitive.

How We Tested

For each scoring configuration, we simulated 3,150 random snake drafts (50 drafts × 7 player counts × 9 seasons) and tracked full leaderboard trajectories episode-by-episode. Wildcards are picked after the first elimination and can overlap other players' draft picks. Sub-drafts ensure every player gets at least 4 picks.

Each configuration was scored on eight metrics, combined into a single composite fun/fairness score (higher = better). Drama and volatility metrics are weighted most heavily:

Rank Volatility Average rank positions each player moves per elimination step Higher = more shuffling3.0
Comeback Rate How often the leader at 50% does NOT end up winning Higher = comebacks happen2.5

Recovery Average final rank of players who lose a pick in the first 3 boots Lower = easier comeback0.5
Final Spread Gap between 1st and last place at the end Smaller = fairer0.5
Non-Draft Impact % of total points from wildcards and Sole Survivor picks Moderate (15–25%)0.5

Parameter space was covered using stratified random sampling (Latin hypercube) with all point values snapped to 0.25 increments.
