How It Works

Each fantasy player builds a roster of Survivor castaways across three phases: the Draft, the Wildcard pick, and optional Replacement picks. Points accumulate as your castaways survive tribal councils, win challenges, find advantages, and advance deeper into the game.

The Draft

Before the season premiere, all fantasy players participate in a snake draft to select their core roster of 4 castaways. If there are more players than the cast can support (e.g. 6+ players for an 18-person cast), the group is split into smaller sub-drafts so everyone still gets 4 picks. Castaways can be shared across sub-drafts. Draft picks earn full points for everything their castaway accomplishes in the game.

Wildcard Pick

After the first episode airs (once the first castaway has been eliminated), each fantasy player selects one Wildcard from any remaining castaway not already on another player's roster.

Wildcards earn half points ({{ "%.0f"|format(config.wildcard_multiplier * 100) }}%) for everything their castaway accomplishes.

Example

If your Wildcard castaway survives 10 tribal councils and earns 12 base points, your Wildcard score would be 6 pts (12 × 50%).

Replacement Picks

After the merge, if one of your picks was eliminated pre-merge, you may select a Replacement from any remaining castaway not already on a roster.

Eligibility

  • You are only eligible for a replacement if one of your picks was eliminated before the merge episode.
  • If a drafted player was eliminated pre-merge, you may pick a full-strength replacement (Replacement).
  • If only your Wildcard was eliminated pre-merge (and no draft picks were), you may pick a wildcard-strength replacement (Replacement ½ pts).
  • If none of your picks were eliminated pre-merge, you are not eligible for any replacement.

If they go on to survive 4 more tribals and win the game, you earn points for those 4 tribals plus any placement/milestone bonuses earned after selection.

Sole Survivor Pick

At the start of the season, each fantasy player predicts who will be the Sole Survivor (the winner of the season). This pick is separate from your draft roster.

{% if config.sole_survivor_val %}

If your Sole Survivor pick wins, you earn {{ config.sole_survivor_val }} point{{ 's' if config.sole_survivor_val != 1 }} per episode in your longest consecutive streak ending at the finale where you had the eventual winner picked as your Sole Survivor.

Example

You pick Player A as your Sole Survivor before the season starts. Player A wins the season ({{ season.num_players if season else 18 }} episodes). If you kept Player A as your pick for the entire season, you'd earn {{ (season.num_players if season else 18) * config.sole_survivor_val }} pts ({{ season.num_players if season else 18 }} episodes × {{ config.sole_survivor_val }}).

If you switched your pick to Player B at Episode 10 but switched back to Player A at Episode 14 (and Player A wins at Episode {{ season.num_players if season else 18 }}), your streak is only {{ (season.num_players if season else 18) - 14 + 1 }} episodes (Ep 14 through the finale), earning {{ ((season.num_players if season else 18) - 14 + 1) * config.sole_survivor_val }} pts.

Same-Day Tribals

Tribal survival is counted per tribal council survived, but when multiple players are eliminated on the same day (double or triple tribal councils), they are all credited with the same number of tribals survived. This ensures fair scoring when the show sends two or three players home in one night.

Example: Double Tribal

In a season where 5 tribals have occurred, a double tribal sends two players home on the same night:

  • Both players survived 5 tribals and earn identical tribal survival points.
  • Players still in the game after the double tribal have now survived 6 tribals (the 5 before + the double tribal night).

On the leaderboard, same-day eliminations are labeled as tied (e.g. "Tied 5th voted out") to indicate shared placement.

Win Probabilities

The leaderboard shows each fantasy player's estimated chance of winning the season.

This is calculated by simulating all possible elimination orders for the remaining castaways:

  • 8 or fewer remaining — all permutations are checked (exhaustive)
  • More than 8 remaining — 50,000 random orderings are sampled

For each simulated outcome, the full leaderboard is recalculated, and the fantasy player who would win is tallied. The win percentage is how often each player comes out on top.

Prediction Modes

Toggle between two modes using the Projected switch in the toolbar:

  • Frozen (default) — uses each castaway's current stats (immunity wins, idols found, etc.) as-is. Only the elimination order is simulated.
  • Projected — adds expected future stats based on historical rates from all 50 US seasons of Survivor. Castaways who survive longer in a simulation are projected to win more immunities, find more idols, etc.
How projected rates work

Individual immunity wins are estimated using data from all 50 US Survivor seasons. Rather than a single average, each castaway's rate is personalized based on their career challenge win history across all seasons they've played. Returning players (e.g., Boston Rob, Parvati) use their full career stats. First-time players use the overall average (~0.13 wins per post-merge tribal survived).

Idol and advantage rates are estimated using only new-era seasons (41+), since the volume of idols and advantages changed dramatically in the modern game.

Idol protection — castaways currently holding a hidden immunity idol have a chance of playing it when they would be eliminated, surviving one additional tribal. The play probability is based on the historical rate of idols being played in new-era seasons.

Data Sources

All game data (boot order, tribe assignments, challenge results, advantages, confessionals) comes from the survivoR open-source dataset, maintained by the community. Player profiles link to survivorstatsdb.com.

Data is automatically refreshed daily at 8am EST.
